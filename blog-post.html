5 Top Reasons Your Business Isn't Getting the Right Attention in 2022

Apr 11, 2022 PR & Branding

Since the early 2000’s, the number of small businesses and budding entrepreneurs have upscaled to over 44 million small businesses within Sub-saharan Africa alone. With the rate at which these businesses are growing, it is very pertinent that your business stands out from the crowd in reaching your core target audience with enough spending power to purchase or use your product. To achieve this, businesses must really be deliberate and intentional about their strategies when it comes to awareness and attention. Like the popular marketing AIDA model states, you have to create the right attention to garner interest, encourage good decision making for the prospect to take action.

We’ve outlined 5 reasons why your business is not getting the right attention and how you can overcome it:

1. Your social media page looks sloppy: A number of businesses are victims to this, because social media is at the second burner of their business, but it actually tells a lot about you. The look and feel and the aesthetic appeal of a social media page helps to tell its own story. It is usually advisable to go with a theme or use a colour grading system that makes the page look right. The content may be great but if not packaged well may not appeal to your audience. So make sure to ensure that both the content and the style in which it is designed or presented look very appealing to draw your audience in and project a persona of class, seriousness and credibility.

2. Your website isn’t saying a lot: Your website does not contain enough details about you! When a prospect view on your website, they want information about you and your business. However, when these things are unavailable, it makes them decide to go elsewhere, where they can find the desired information that they require. The way to go over this is to write out quality content that answers the possible questions that your clients or prospects may have such that it is easier for them to trust and work with your business. Basic questions like what you do, your service hours and more should be adequately displayed on your website as well as in depth information on the services that you provide.

3. Zero digital footprint almost equals Zero Credibility: You do not have a digital footprint that adds credibility to your business. Therefore, having a credible brand is very important for your business, it can make or mar you in terms of trust for your business and convincing leads to become buyers. Therefore, it is advisable to have your company name mentioned in credible blogs and news sites. Your key leadership team can also be featured in relevant newspapers and press releases could be shared with the media to add that much needed spice. You never know who is watching or ‘Googling’ your business, so make it a date to always have a good digital footprint.

4. You do good work, but Nobody knows: We have seen businesses who are doing really good work but no one knows because they don't publicise their wins. For conservative brands, you must have built a level of brand equity over the years to be on the quiet side; but if your brand is at the growth stage, where you are supposed to be building a business, it is imperative to announce wins and successes. This does not mean that you should make noise talking about your business without relevance, but it means that you should strategically and intentionally put your brand out there. Celebrate your wins using a preferred media channel and ensure that your brand promise is congruent in everything that you do.

5. Your message is always in zig-zags: This means that your message is not consistent: In spite of putting your business out there, it is also noteworthy to discuss your brand messaging. When a brand speaks different lingos or uses different tones at each interval, then it causes inconsistency in the brand. Trust us, as human beings, people like consistency and are not very open to immediate change. Therefore, having a synchronised message will help transcend your business to the next level. By messaging we mean, what is that unique statement or distinct take-away that you want your audience to get from any interaction with your brand. It could be that the brand shares happiness always and other emotions like Coca-Cola, or the brand believes in sponsoring Youth and entertainment like Pepsi. Always ensure that people leave your brand knowing your brand essence and messaging because that’s the whole point of effective communication.

We’ve highlighted 5 tips why your business is not getting attention as well as 5 reasons why it deserves to get that attention. Make sure to implement these in your day to day activities and plan for different occasions and seasons.
